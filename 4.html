Principal Investigator:

Muhammad Shaikh Hassan


Project Website: Tobacco Control Project

My Position: Principal Investigator (PI)

Summary of the Project

This research project was funded by a tobacco control research grants program as part of a graduate student research initiative. The project aligned with the Bloomberg Initiative's priority to reduce tobacco use by generating evidence-based insights. Additionally, the project contributed to capacity-building workshops for grantees, covering the entire research process and facilitating the dissemination of findings to policymakers.

Objectives: The primary objective of this project was to investigate the smoking pattern, socio-demographic determinants, and health-related problems regarding tobacco consumption among university students in Sylhet Division.

Study Setting: The study was conducted among university students from government and private universities in Sylhet Division, Bangladesh.

Design and Methods: The study employed a quantitative cross-sectional design to assess the prevalence of tobacco use. A structured face-to-face interview-based data collection method was used to gather information on smoking behaviors and related factors.

Skill Set Required for This Project

  • Study Design and Protocol Development: Designed the study protocol, methodology, and sampling strategy to ensure robust data collection.
  • Data Collection and Training: Developed standardized data collection tools, provided training to data collectors, and monitored the entire data collection process.
  • Data Analysis: Performed statistical analysis, including descriptive analysis and logistic regression analysis, to identify predictors of tobacco smoking among university students.
  • Reporting and Dissemination: Wrote the final report and manuscripts for publication. Presented key research findings at international conferences to contribute to global tobacco control discussions.
