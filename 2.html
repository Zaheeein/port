Scientific Evaluation of the Psychosocial Impacts of Baby Friendly Spaces in Low-Resource Humanitarian Settings

Background Information

Lead Organization: Action Contre La Faim / Action Against Hunger

Funded by: United States Agency for International Development (USAID)

Principal Investigators:

Dr. Karine Le Roch

Dr. Sarah Murray


Project Website: ACF Bangladesh

My Position: Qualitative Survey Manager

Summary of the Project

Depression symptoms during pregnancy and postpartum are widespread globally. In humanitarian crises, factors such as poverty and exposure to violence exacerbate risks to child health and development. These environmental stressors increase the likelihood of cognitive delays, mental health disorders, physical illness, and malnutrition in children. Additionally, caregivers of young children face significant mental and physical health challenges, which can reduce their ability to shield their children from these adverse effects.

Objectives: This study assessed the effectiveness of the Baby Friendly Spaces (BFS) intervention in improving the psychosocial well-being of conflict-affected mothers and enhancing child development and growth. The research focused on Rohingya mothers and their children residing in refugee camps in Cox’s Bazar, Bangladesh.

Study Setting: Refugee camps in Cox’s Bazar, Bangladesh

Design and Methods: A cluster randomized controlled trial (RCT) was conducted to evaluate the BFS program, which was developed by Action Against Hunger (ACF). The intervention included three individual counseling sessions along with five individual or group-based psychosocial stimulation sessions. A mixed-methods approach was applied to assess its impact, utilizing quantitative measures to evaluate distress levels, functional abilities, subjective well-being, and positive coping strategies. Additionally, both qualitative and quantitative methods were used to examine the implementation process.

Skill Set Required for This Project

  • Qualitative Data Collection: Conducted in-depth interviews (IDIs) with service providers in both refugee camps and host communities, as well as with key stakeholders such as government officials, ACF policymakers, and protection sector personnel.
  • Field Observations: Assessed the fidelity of ACF’s service providers within Rohingya camps using participant observation methods to evaluate service delivery and adherence to intervention protocols.
  • Data Management and Analysis: Transcribed and translated IDI audio recordings, monitored and tracked data collection progress, and conducted thematic analysis to extract key insights from qualitative data.
  • Reporting and Knowledge Dissemination: Contributed to report writing, project briefs, and blogs to communicate research findings. Assisted in manuscript preparation for academic publications.
