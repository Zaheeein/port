About Me

I am a dedicated public health researcher with extensive experience in epidemiology, qualitative research, and social and behavioural science. Currently, I am working as a Research Investigator at the International Centre for Diarrheal Disease Research, Bangladesh (icddr,b), focusing on community engagement activities under the Child Health and Mortality Prevention Surveillance (CHAMPS) project, funded by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.

With a strong background in mixed-method research, I have contributed to multiple large-scale research projects in Bangladesh, focusing on perinatal mortality, antimicrobial resistance, and healthcare governance. My work has been instrumental in informing policies and improving public health interventions.

I hold a Master of Medical Science in Public Health from the University of Malaya, Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, and a Master’s in Anthropology from Shahjalal University of Science & Technology, Sylhet, Bangladesh. My scholarly contributions have been published in leading peer-reviewed journals.
