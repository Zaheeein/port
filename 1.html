Summary of the Project

The CHAMPS (Child Health and Mortality Prevention Surveillance) network operates in eight countries across Africa and South Asia, focusing on regions with high child mortality rates. The initiative seeks to deepen the understanding of the causes of child deaths by generating critical evidence that informs effective health interventions. CHAMPS prioritizes community-driven approaches, fostering local ownership, strengthening healthcare infrastructure, and ensuring that collected data translates into impactful public health measures.

Objectives: The primary goal of the project is to determine the causes of stillbirths and child mortality through community engagement, mortality surveillance, advanced diagnostic techniques, and demographic health monitoring.

Study Setting: Baliakandi Upazila, Rajbari District, Bangladesh

Methods and Activities: The project employs Minimally Invasive Tissue Sampling (MITS) â€” a post-mortem diagnostic procedure designed to accurately identify causes of death. Additionally, the Social and Behavioural Science (SBS) component is integral to the project, as it involves conducting qualitative rapid assessments and engaging with the community to build trust and ensure the acceptability of CHAMPS activities. These efforts help align project initiatives with local values, concerns, and expectations, ensuring long-term sustainability and impact in Bangladesh.

Skill Set Required for This Project

  • Qualitative Data Analysis: Conducted thematic analysis using NVivo and MAXQDA software to analyze interview transcripts, focus group discussions, and community feedback session notes.
  • Quantitative Data Analysis: Used statistical software such as SPSS, Stata, and R to assess trends, identify associations, and evaluate maternal and child health indicators.
  • Community Engagement & Rumor Investigation: Identified, analyzed, and addressed community concerns, rumors, and misconceptions surrounding CHAMPS activities, particularly related to the MITS in Baliakandi.
