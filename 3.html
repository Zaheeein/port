Irregularities, Informal Practices, and the Motivation of Frontline Healthcare Providers in Bangladesh

Background Information

Funded by: UKaid

Principal Investigator:

Professor Mushtaq Khan


Project Website: ACE Bangladesh

My Position: Senior Research Associate

Summary of the Project

Corruption in the healthcare sector can significantly impact the accessibility, quality, effectiveness, and fairness of healthcare services. It also obstructs the successful execution of initiatives aimed at improving health systems. Since healthcare expenditures constitute a substantial portion of government budgets, the sector remains vulnerable to corruption due to imbalances in information, uncertainty regarding resource allocation, and challenges in establishing effective accountability mechanisms.

Objectives: This project looks for sectoral anti-corruption strategies using ‘horizontal’ support for enforcement in critical sectors like health, power generation, land markets, skills delivery, industrial regulation, and infrastructure investments.

Study Setting: Upazila health complex in Barisal, Patuakhali, Sylhet, and Sunamganj District, Bangladesh

Design and Methods: This study employed a qualitative study design to explore corruption in the health sector, its impact on service delivery, and potential policy solutions. In-depth Interviews (IDIs) with frontline healthcare providers to understand their experiences and challenges related to corruption. Key Informant Interviews (KIIs) with policymakers and government officials to assess governance mechanisms and policy gaps. Observations of healthcare service provision at Upazila Health Complexes to identify informal practices, resource misallocation, and inefficiencies. Moreover, Discrete Choice Experiment (DCE) was performed to assess potential solutions for reducing corruption and enhancing accountability in the healthcare sector.

Skill Set Required for This Project

  • Systematic Review: Conducted a scoping review on health-sector corruption and relevant national policies. Developed a review protocol, outlining key research questions, inclusion/exclusion criteria, and data sources, using the PRISMA framework to ensure a rigorous and structured review process.
  • Qualitative Data Analysis: Designed qualitative data collection guidelines for conducting in-depth interviews with frontline healthcare providers and key informant interviews with policymakers and government officials. Observed healthcare service activities at Upazila Health Complexes to assess governance and service quality. Conducted data transcription, translation, coding, and analysis using the framework analysis method to extract key themes and insights.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Coordinated and maintained liaison with key stakeholders in the health sector in Bangladesh. Organized policy forums, policy dialogue workshops, and roundtable discussions on health-sector corruption and potential solutions.
  • Project Dissemination: Presented research findings at the Fifth Global Symposium on Health Systems Research (HSR2018) in Liverpool, UK. Participated in a panel discussion on corruption in the health sector, organized by the ACE research consortium, to share insights and advocate for policy improvements.
